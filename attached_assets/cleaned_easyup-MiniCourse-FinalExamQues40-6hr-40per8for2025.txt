1. Richard anticipates reporting a taxable income of $210,000 for 2025. When he files his federal income taxes as a single taxpayer, of the following marginal income tax rate brackets, he would be taxed at the:
a. 22% rate.
b. 24% rate.
c. 32% rate.
d. 35% rate.
2. In 2025, Sam expects to earn $315,200 and anticipates filing married jointly. Given this information, what would be the status of his personal exemptions since the enactment of the Tax Cuts & Jobs Act (TCJA)?
a. His personal exemptions would remain in place without regard to AGI.
b. His personal exemptions would be zero.
c. His personal exemptions would be reduced by 2% of each $2,500 in excess of AGI above a threshold amount.
d. His personal exemptions would be reduced by 4% of each $2,500 in excess of AGI above a threshold amount.
3. Mike is an upper-income taxpayer and itemizes but, his deductions have always been reduced by the 3% overall limitation under §68. In 2025, the TCJA might offer Mike some relief because it:
a. temporarily suspended the overall limitation on itemized deductions.
b. doubled the number of itemized deductions for individuals.
c. provides that §68 only applies to corporations.
d. increased the standard deduction to include itemized deductions.

4. Erwin is an enrolled agent operating his tax business as a sole proprietor. Recently, he has been busy listing deductions for his clients that have been suspended by the TCJA. However, which of the following suspended deductions is most likely to affect Erwin's relationship with his clients:
a. hobby expenses.
b. tax preparation expenses.
c. investment fees.
d. trustee’s fees for an IRA.
5. William is single and will earn about $75,000 in 2025. He asks how the TCJA would affect his AMT exemption. You should inform him that:
a. his AMT exemption has been increased to $88,100 for 2025.
b. because of William’s earnings, his AMT exemption amount will be subject to a 25% phase-out. 
c. his AMT exemption must be patched periodically by Congress, it is not indexed to inflation.
d. his AMT exemption was completely eliminated for 2020 and thereafter.
6. Carol and David, two moderate-income clients with two young children, want to know how recent legislation affects their use of the child tax credit. You should explain that it:
a. makes the 2021 increased child tax credit permanent.
b. modified the credit to only apply to 15% of earned income in excess of $8,500.
c. returns the credit to $2,000 per qualifying child for 2025.
d. repeals the child tax credit.
7. Marge will be divorced in 2025 and expects to receive substantial alimony payments. When she meets with her tax advisor, she explains to Marge that under the TCJA such payments will be treated as:
a. qualified dependency support.
b. not includable in her income as the payee.
c. deductible by the payor.
d. a gift to the recipient.

8. You are on a tax panel for a group of homeowners. One individual wants to know what kind of items would most likely enable him to fully qualify for the home mortgage interest deduction in 2025. You inform him that one such item would be a:
a. $750,000 purchase money loan on a residence.
b. $100,000 in home equity indebtedness.
c. debt used to buy real estate.
d. second mortgage.
9. Fred plans to send his young daughter to parochial elementary and secondary school. However, it will be expensive and he is looking for ways to save on the tuition. You should inform Fred that, under the TCJA, §529 savings plans:
a. can only cover qualified higher education expenses.
b. are not available for private schools.
c. are suspended for 5 years.
d. can now cover religious elementary or secondary school expenses.
10. Scott obtained a student loan of $40,000 to finish his last year at Caltech and become an engineer. In 2025, he was totally and permanently disabled while working on a project and can no longer make payments. Under the TCJA, he should consider dealing with the student loan by:
a. filing bankruptcy.
b. seeking a loan discharge, since it will not be included in his income.
c. seeking additional financial assistance.
d. having Medicare pay the loan.
11. Dan is age 58 and wants to maximize his Health Savings Account (HSA), for which he has individual coverage. Under these circumstances, Dan can make his standard contribution in 2025 plus an additional contribution of:
a. $900.
b. $1,000.
c. $2,400.
d. $2,500.
12. In 2025, Joan placed solar electric & water heating property into service. Subject to certain limits, Joan should be eligible for a personal §25D credit tax of:
a. 10%.
b. 20%.
c. 30%.
d. 40%.
13. Peter is deliberating upon what type of entity he wants for his small computer systems design firm. He is thinking about organizing as an S corporation and seeks your advice. You would explain to him that he might find it beneficial to organize as a C corporation since:
a. a C corporation allows shareholders to deduct personal losses.
b. a C corporation distributes its profits to shareholders with a single tax.
c. a C corporation eliminates unreasonable compensation problems.
d. the TCJA has reduced the general C corporate tax rate to 21%.
14. Jerry wants to promote his business with some great entertainment targeting potential customers. As his advisor, you should warn him of the following general category expense which is disallowed as a result of the TCJA:
a. food associated with operating a business.
b. beverages associated with operating a business.
c. first class travel.
d. all activities considered to be entertainment.
15. With many employee "perks" being curtailed by the TCJA, John is frustrated in his search for tax-favored benefits to offer his employees. You should explain to him that §45F still allows:
a. an expanded bicycle commuting reimbursement exclusion.
b. a credit for employee entertainment facilities.
c. a credit for childcare assistance.
d. an improved moving expense reimbursement exclusion.
16. Under §168(k), an additional first-year depreciation deduction is allowed based on a percentage of the adjusted basis of qualified property. However, by such rules, which of the following is an example of nonqualified property?
a. used property.
b. off-the-shelf computer software.
c. tangible property depreciated under MACRS with a recovery period of 20 years
d. business property acquired and converted to personal use in the same year.
17. Delbert has chosen to purchase and expense used equipment he will place in service in his business. Under §179, he may elect to treat the permitted expensing amount as:
a. a deduction.
b. a 25% per year tax credit.
c. capitalized.
d. an amortization of the equipment’s cost.
18. In the final months of 2024, Jerry used his pick-up truck to make deliveries from the nursery he owns and operates. He kept a careful record of all his business miles. Given this, he should be able to claim a standard mileage rate of:
a. 65.5 cents per mile.
b. 67 cents per mile.
c. 68.5 cents per mile.
d. 70 cents per mile.
19. Assume you are a single taxpayer and anticipate earning $70,000 as an employee in 2025. The rate used to calculate your portion of OASDI will be:
a. 6.2%.
b. 4.2%.
c. 10.4%.
d. 12.4%.
20. Mike, a builder, has just completed a low-income housing project. Which of the following statements is correct regarding the Low-Income Housing Tax Credit (LIHTC) under §42 for this project?
a. The tax credit can be claimed annually for 10 years, based on a percentage of the qualified basis of the low-income building.
b. Mike can claim the entire tax credit amount in the first year after the building is placed in service.
c. Mike must wait 15 years before claiming any tax credits for the project.
d. The tax credit amount remains constant regardless of the qualified basis of the building.
21. Fred, a real estate investor, is considering exchanging one of his investment properties. Which of the following scenarios would qualify for a tax-deferred like-kind exchange under Section 1031?
a. Fred exchanges his vacant land for a commercial office building.
b. Fred exchanges his apartment building for a collection of rare coins.
c. Fred exchanges his rental property for shares in a real estate investment trust.
d. Fred exchanges his investment property for a luxury yacht to use for personal vacations.

22. Jim owns an auto dealership in California but, his daughter Jane lives in Paris with John, her French boyfriend. To avoid U.S. taxation, John cannot conduct any U.S. trade or business. As a result, under the TCJA, John should not:
a. marry Jane.
b. visit the United States for more than 30 days.
c. buy a vehicle from Jim.
d. buy a partnership interest in Jim's business.
23. You are reviewing the numerous detailed insurance changes made by the TCJA with your client, Dianne, who has just started a small life insurance company. The provision most likely to have an immediate impact on her young company is: 
a.  the change in the computation of reserves.
b. the repeal of the special deduction for small life insurance companies.
c. the special distribution rule for pre-1984 accounts.
d. the tax reporting of life settlement transactions.
24. Mike is a client and a director of a large tax-exempt organization that has recently come under media attack for excessive compensation. You should alert Mike that under the TCJA an exempt organization can:
a. make tax-exempt qualified equity grants to employees.
b. use performance-based compensation.
c. be liable for a 21% excise tax on compensation over $1 million.
d. not use parachute payments.
25. Frank is the trustee of a large private college with a majority of U.S. tuition-paying students. Over the years, you have helped him navigate the complex tax rules surrounding such institutions. Because of the TCJA, you should schedule a meeting with Frank to discuss the TCJA's :
a. imposition of a 1.4% excise tax on net investment income.
b. repeal of the unrelated business income tax.
c. exemption of college and university investment income.
d. provision of a $100,000 standard deduction.
26. Dr. Willis Johnson anticipates his compensation to be $320,000 in 2025. As a result, his maximum deductible contribution to a defined contribution retirement plan for 2025 should be:
a. $61,000.
b. $66,000.
c. $69,000.
d. $70,000.
27. Philip is married and filing jointly. As a result, Philip cannot contribute to a Roth IRA in 2025 if his modified AGI reaches:
a. $208,000.
b. $214,000.
c. $240,000.
d. $246,000.
28. After his father's recent death, Ross received a distribution from his inherited IRA. As a result, you should advise Ross that:
a. Non-spouse beneficiaries can only convert from either a traditional, SEP, or SIMPLE IRA into a Roth IRA.
b. When a participant dies, plan distributions transfer into a surviving spouse’s IRA tax-free but are taxed when transferred to a non-spouse’s IRA.
c. A 401(k) cannot offer a direct rollover of a distribution to a non-spouse beneficiary, which will not be included in the beneficiary’s income in the year of the rollover.
d. Distributions to a non-spouse beneficiary’s inherited IRA are considered eligible rollover distributions, and plans must allow the beneficiaries to make these direct rollovers.
29. Ralph and Jan, a married couple, have been considering a marital deduction trust for several years to save on estate taxes. However, they may wish to reconsider their plan in light of the:
a. elimination of marital deduction trusts.
b. suspension of the individual alternative minimum tax.
c. repeal of estate taxes.
d. recent increases in the applicable exclusion amount.
30. Your client, Ralph, is the trustee of an irrevocable trust, and pursuant to the trust terms, he has elected to retain $13,000 of net income in the trust for 2025. As a result, you warn Ralph that such income will be taxed at a rate of:
a. 10%.
b. 24%.
c. 35%.
d. 37%.
